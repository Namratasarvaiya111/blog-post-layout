There's no one right way to build a creative business — especially now

There are people with zero social media presence who make millions selling books. Others have impactful businesses and stay below six figures. On purpose. Some people want to be as lazy as possible and make tons of money by sending emails. Sounds unrealistic. But maybe not.

Many of us are conditioned to believe there's a “right” way to build a business. Sure, there are well-worn paths that might work. But fulfillment? Or a unique expression of your gifts and spirit? That's a different game.

What makes those unconventional creators stand out isn't just the deviation from the norm — it's their willingness to ignore outside pressure and listen to their inner knowing. They design businesses and workflows around who they are, not who they're told to be.

And yet, so many of us struggle to do the same. Why?

Because we're taught to distrust our instincts. We're taught to fear failure. So we chase templates and tactics instead of trusting our creative compass.

This post advocates for self-trust. For tuning in rather than out. Especially in a world that's moving faster than ever.
